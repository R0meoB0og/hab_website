Detecting HABs Using Deep Learning & Satellite Imagery




Master Thesis - Romain Huet


Traditional methods of Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs) monitoring are done in-situ by collecting samples of water and analyzing their composition. These methods are time-consuming and hardly scalable as they require a lot of resources, either financial or manpower. Satellites can remedy these problems as data is widely available, free, and of high space & time resolutions. Using Sentinel 3 OLCI satellite images and a U-Net framework (a type of Convolutional Neural Network), it is possible to predict pixels of a satellite image that contain HABs. The first image shown is the original natural color image. Second is the label, which labels in white pixels of the original image that display HABs. Last, the model generates a prediction, without knowing the label, for which pixels of the original image correspond to HABs. The U-Net model used to generate the predictions above was trained on Sentinel 3 OLCI images of Lake Okeechobee in 2021. Its performance is satisfactory for new images of Lake Okeechobee in 2022, though it struggles to accurately predict HABs for images of Sanigaw Bay in 2025.
